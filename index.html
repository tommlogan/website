Tom's Research Interests

I am a doctoral student with Dr. Seth Guikema in the Department of Industrial and Operations Engineering at the University of Michigan. I'm interested in sustainability and resilience, especially in urban systems, as well as ecosystems and their stability. My research revolves around these topics, applying tools such as

  • statistical learning
  • risk analysis
  • network theory
  • mathematical modelling.

Projects

Prediction models

Tom M. Logan, Stella McLeod, Seth Guikema

Reliable pre-harvest predictions of fruit size would be useful to growers. Unfortunately, some proposed models have not been tested accurately, while other models have seen varying degrees of success. We demonstrate appropriate methods for the construction and testing of a predictive model using apple data.

Shearwater migration

Tom M. Logan

Shearwaters migrate huge distances every year, from the Arctic to the Antarctic and back! These seabirds live an amazing life of endless summer - but how do they know when to depart?

Tsunami and behaviour modelling

Tom M. Logan, Seth Guikema

In areas with a history of tsunamis, how do people's choices about where to live and work affect a city's vulnerability?

Mutualism networks and family trees

Alex James, Tom M. Logan, Michael J. Plank, Jeanette McLeod

Have mutualistic (beneficial) ecological interactions developed randomly, or is there an underlying explanation for their structure? We investigate whether phylogenetic structure influences beneficial interactions.

Predator-prey networks

Tom M. Logan, Alex James

Are the predator-prey networks observed in the wild completely random? Are the interactions random? This project investigated some of the patterns behind predator-prey network architecture.

Heavy metals in urban streams

Tom M. Logan, Daniel Risi, Tom Cochrane

Rain gardens? Permeable pavements? Green roofs? We developed a model to determine the optimal deployment of green technologies in an urban catchment/watershed with an objective of reducing heavy metal contamination in the stream.
